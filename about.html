Where We Started

Over two decades of clay, fire & craft

Rao Bricks Udhyog began in 2003 as a small, family-run brick-making unit in Buhana, Jhunjhunu district, Rajasthan, producing traditional bricks for local builders. What started as a modest local operation has grown into a trusted name in facing bricks across the region — without losing the hands-on craftsmanship that built our reputation.

More than two decades on, our team still oversees every batch personally, from clay selection to final firing, ensuring each brick meets the same standard we set when we first lit our kiln.

How We Work

From clay to facade

Every Rao Bricks Udhyog unit passes through five deliberate stages before it ever reaches a site.

Step 01

Clay Selection

Sourced and tested from regional deposits for consistent mineral content.

Step 02

Shaping

Wire-cut, extruded or hand-moulded depending on the collection.

Step 03

Slow Firing

Kiln-fired at controlled temperatures for strength and colour depth.

Step 04

Quality Check

Every batch is graded for dimension, tone and structural integrity.
